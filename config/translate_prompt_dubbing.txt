Task: Translate movie subtitles into {target_language} for AI dubbing. Each line is spoken by a cloned voice and must fit its original on-screen time slot, so the translation's natural spoken length should be close to the original line's spoken duration.

The subtitles are the complete dialogue of one video, listed in playback order (subtitle_id increases in playback order), and were produced by speech recognition (ASR), so they may contain recognition errors.

Core Constraint: Match the spoken duration (both directions)
- Aim for a translation whose natural spoken length is close to the original line's spoken length.
  * Too LONG -> the dub has to rush or overruns into the next line.
  * Too SHORT -> the dub finishes early and leaves audible silence in the gap.
- Timing tolerance: the dubbing engine fine-tunes the speaking speed by about ±15%, so you have a small margin on both sides. Do NOT over-compress or drop meaning just to be short — prefer a faithful, natural line whose spoken length lands close to the original.
- Length anchors (depend on the source language; use as a rough guide, not a hard rule):
  * Japanese -> Chinese: 1 kanji/kana ≈ 0.6–0.8 Chinese characters.
  * Japanese -> English: 1 kanji/kana ≈ 0.3–0.4 English words.
  * Any other source/target pair: judge by natural spoken length rather than character count — the line should take about as long to say out loud as the original.
- Register & pace: use natural, spoken, colloquial language and contractions; avoid stiff, formal, or long multi-syllabic words that are hard to say quickly, especially for rapid original lines.

Context & Consistency (important):
- Always use the surrounding subtitles as context. A sentence often spans several consecutive subtitles; the dubbed lines must connect naturally and read as one continuous dialogue.
- Keep person names, nicknames, forms of address, and recurring terms consistent across the whole file.
- Keep each speaker's tone and register consistent across the whole file.
- The source text is ASR output: it may contain homophone mistakes, wrong particles, or garbled fragments. Infer the intended meaning from the context and translate that intended meaning, not the literal mis-recognized words.

Content Background:
    Contains adult elements; therefore, the translation will primarily use informal, spoken-style language suitable.

--------

Instructions:
1. Format Integrity: Each subtitle must start with <subtitle_id> and end with </subtitle_id>. Maintain a strict one-to-one correspondence.
2. Contextual Coherence: Understand the dialogue flow to ensure the translation is natural, but never merge or split IDs.
3. Handle Noise: Ignore gibberish or errors; if a subtitle_text is empty or nonsensical, output <subtitle_id></subtitle_id>. NEVER echo the source text or leave source-language words in the output — every non-empty output line must be written entirely in {target_language} (a cloned voice has to speak it).
4. Lip-Sync Optimization (for Chinese target): Avoid ending sentences with long, dragging particles unless the original speaker does so.

Input Format:
Subtitles are between START and END markers.
Example: <id>text</id>
subtitle_id increases in playback order: neighboring subtitles are consecutive lines of the same dialogue.

Length Reference Guide (for {target_language}):
- Short original (e.g. "さあ") -> an equally punchy translation (e.g. "来吧" or "So"). Do not pad it into a full sentence.
- Rapid-fire original -> avoid heavy multi-syllabic words that are hard to speak quickly.
- Do not compress a normal line into a clipped fragment either — keep the length proportional to the original so the dub neither rushes nor leaves silent gaps.

Input:

Output (Optimized for Sync):
START
<1>当然会说“会”。-为啥？但这不代表就会买。</1>
<2>来。</2>
END

Subtitles to Translate:
-----------------------
START

{subtitles}

Remember: The goal is synchronized dubbing. Match the original line's spoken duration (neither much longer nor much shorter), keep a natural spoken rhythm, use the surrounding lines as context, and keep names, terms, and tone consistent across the whole file. Never skip or merge IDs.
